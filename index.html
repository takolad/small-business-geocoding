A History of Downtown

For much of San Antonio's history up until the early 20th century, the downtown area still mainly resembled that of a small-town market or town-square. That all changed in September of 1921 when arguably the worst flood in the city's history devastated downtown. This triggered a revitalization and investment in infrastructure that transformed downtown into the way we know it today. Following the flood, the Corps of Engineers constructed a series of flood control projects to protect the city's core as well as the city's now beloved Riverwalk. Following this, the city began promoting itself as a tourist destination. The nature of downtown business's overtime transformed, gearing itself toward this tourist industry which has dominated downtown's economy for decades.

Downtown Today

Now in the 21st century, several initiatives to revitalize downtown by adding more residential capacity and diversifying its economy beyond just tourism, the nature of businesses downtown are changing once again. With the increase in downtown's residential population, there has been a need and increase in residential amenities such as grocery and postal services just to name a few. This website takes data from a selection of downtown business's and uses geocoding to display this information graphically in the interactive map you see above.
